In the real world I inhabit Phoenix, Arizona, USA. Between five and ten weekday evenings I can typically be spotted near N33° 29.0744′, W112° 5.3194′.

When I’m not there you can find me online, often lurking in an IRC channel, reading or writing technical documentation, pushing commits to open source projects on GitHub, or digging through the depths of information scattered throughout the Web. If all else fails, look for me at the dog park reading an analog book near a golden and a catahoula.

This site does not exist for me to sell you something. In the spirit of the Hacker Ethic, nearly all of my projects are released under open source licensing so that all can reuse, learn from, and contribute to my projects..

Should you find an invalid or outdated statement on my site or in my code, consider taking the time to refute it and set me straight. Let’s work together to keep the internet free of garbage and noise.


I was built in San Francisco around 1985 and crossed over into the virtual world, using Netscape Navigator and a text editor, around the time Geocities was popular . My hobbies include spending time with my non-human animal friends, indie and electronic dance music, literature, the flying spaghetti monster, cooking, science, puns, science fiction, the Internet, and programming for the latter.

Around one year ago I split up with the quad-core AMD Phenom II Black Edition to which I was practically married for five years. We may still love each other, but the world is taking us in different directions . For the moment I live in Phoenix, Arizona, among our old memories.

“Chris Vogt” is just my mundane name; you can call me “C1V0″.
